If you are reading this, you were probably invited here by someone on stage right now.

GigMan Request lets you make requests of the performer. You can also let them know, with a down-vote, if you really don't like a particular song. This can be very useful to the artist, as it lets them know what is working, and what is not.
Making your request is as easy as 1-2-3-4-5:
  • Click one of the Request boxes ('Request One', 'Request Two', 'Request Three' etc.) to make it active. The active Request box has a bold/italic title.
  • Browse the list of songs until you find one of your favorites (or, if the artist has enabled downvotes) one you really dislike).
  • Click the up-vote icon () or the down-vote icon ().
  • Repeat, up to (depending on how the artist has set things up) 5 more times.
  • Click the Done! button.
Once your requests are made and sent, they go to our servers, which push them to the performer, where they can read the request results on their wireless device.

It should be noted that, while you can pick up to 5 (depending on how the artist has set things up) songs, you can only make one request per song.

That's it!

GigMan Messages allows you to send prepared and personalized messages to the band or individual band members.
You can even add an attachment to your message!
To send a message:
  • Select a topic from the drop down list.
  • Add some (optional) text of your own in the text box.
  • Add an (optional) attachment file to your message.
  • Repeat up to (depending on how the artist has set things up) 5 more times.
  • Click the Done! button.
Like the requests, messages are sent to the servers and then pushed to the performer in near real time.
